Hi, I'm Lewis. I am an upcoming PhD graduate in Statistics (December 2021) from the Department of Applied Mathematics and Statistics at Colorado School of Mines, USA. I develop spatial statistics tools for analyzing massive spatial data sets to study the Earth system.

A primary component of my PhD has been as a Research Assistant at the National Center for Atmospheric Research (NCAR) in Boulder, Colorado, USA. There I developed and released scalable machine learning models which allow users to compute with hundreds-of-millions of observations and reduced computation times by 75%. Additionally, I produced foundational work for future research by performing statistical analysis of massive satellite data sets on the Cheyenne and Casper HPC systems, identifying global patterns and trends. The experience I garnered in these projects and my studies has solidified my passion for applying computational science and statistics to atmospheric and climate science, and this is a line of work I want to continue. I am currently in search of positions where I can apply my skills to develop a career at the interface of computational science, statistics, and the atmospheric sciences.
